When I was young man, I remember seeing some of the greatest air guitarists in the world perform on stage in my small town of Lindon, TN. That was back when candy bars were a $0.35 a piece and gas was under a dollar a gallon — those were the golden years of the art known as the air guitar.

I remember seeing these pros and asking myself “How do they do such greatness without a guitar in hand?” I would practice hours upon hours each day, month after month, year after year. Yes, I would get better, but I still was lacking something and I could not quite “put my finger on it”, per say.

That is until I came across a good friend of mine from our youth, Gary. We had spent many hours together, jamming away on our air guitars up until he had moved from out of the state. A few years later, here we were standing in line at the local grocery store when he asked me how my playing was going.

I told Gary that I had hit a rut and couldn't quite get out of it. I'll never forget what he told me. He said, “Lawrence, you've got talent — I've seen you rock it like it's nobody's business. But, you are missing the key ingredient: cliche. To be different from everybody else, you have to truly try to be like everybody else.”

That was it!

You've got to be cliche to standout!

This made all the difference, but there was still more…

After our reunion, I went home and practiced and practiced. After 3 months of trial and error, I unlocked the secrets and quickly found where this could take me. Being cliche certainly was the the tipping ingredient that got me on the right path, but there still was so much more…

I had the privilege of mentoring some of the most well known artists in the air guitar rock and roll world.
